# Vesra Partner ICP

## Current Partner Focus

The Vesra partner system supports multiple ICP profiles. Active profiles live in
`lead-gen/config/icp_profiles.json`.

Current active ICPs:

- HR consultancy partners.
- Franchise/franchisor partners.

Previous broader segments, such as accountancies and employment law firms, are
parked for now and should not be included in the active outbound queue unless a
future campaign explicitly reopens them.

## Franchise Networks ICP

### Target Partner

UK franchise networks with multiple locations, a central head office, and a
growing distributed workforce that needs better visibility and consistency
across people processes.

### Criteria

- UK based.
- Established franchise brand.
- 3-150 locations.
- Central head office.
- Growing network.
- 50+ employees across the network.
- Distributed workforce.
- Multiple locations to manage.
- Existing operational processes but limited visibility across locations.
- No modern HR platform deployed consistently across the network.

### Operational Challenges

- Holiday management.
- Sickness tracking.
- Employee records.
- Policy distribution and acknowledgements.
- Onboarding new employees.
- Compliance management.
- Visibility across locations.
- Consistency between franchisees.
- Reporting and oversight.
- Scaling operations as the network grows.

### Why They Are Good Partners

- Head office often lacks visibility into day-to-day people management across
  the network.
- Processes become inconsistent as new locations are added.
- Administrative workload increases with growth.
- Franchisees often use different systems or spreadsheets.
- Vesra provides a central platform that standardises people management across
  the network.

### Ideal Size

- 250-2,000 employees across the network.

### Sweet Spot

- 3-100 locations.
- 50-1,000 employees.

### Exclusions

Exclude from the franchise partner campaign:

- Single-location businesses.
- Owner-operator franchises.
- Consultancy/coaching franchises with few employees.
- Networks with fewer than 50 employees.
- Franchise directories.
- Franchise resale listings.
- Franchise consultants selling advisory services to would-be franchisors.
- Franchise solicitors/law firms.
- Recruitment franchises.
- Job boards.
- Pure software providers.

### Mission

Help franchise networks scale with confidence.

### Core Value Proposition

Give head office visibility and consistency across every location.

### Outcomes

- Better operational control.
- Greater visibility across the network.
- Lower administrative burden.
- Reduced compliance risk.
- Faster onboarding.
- More consistent employee experience.
- Easier network growth and expansion.

### Belief

Growing franchise networks need modern systems to maintain consistency,
visibility, and control as they scale.

### Target Verticals

- Home care franchises.
- Children's activity franchises.
- Estate agency franchises.
- Trades franchises.
- Cleaning franchises.
- Hospitality franchises.

### Decision Makers

Prioritize:

- Founder.
- Owner.
- Managing Director.
- Franchise Director.
- Head of Franchise.
- Operations Director.
- People Director.

### Suggested Scoring

Score each company from 0 to 100:

- Franchise/network segment fit: 0-25.
- Multi-site or franchisee evidence: 0-20.
- Operational/compliance support evidence: 0-15.
- Company/network size fit: 0-10.
- Decision-maker clarity: 0-10.
- Contactability: 0-10.
- Partnership relevance: 0-10.

## Target Partner

HR consultancies that already have retained SME client relationships and can add Vesra as a branded HR software layer alongside their advice, policies, documentation, and people support.

## Criteria

- UK based.
- 2-50 employees.
- 20+ active clients.
- Ongoing retained client relationships.
- Provides outsourced HR support.
- Advises SMEs on people, compliance, and employment matters.
- Looking to grow recurring revenue.
- Does not have its own software platform.

## Services

Best-fit partners usually provide several of these:

- HR advice.
- Employee relations.
- Policies and handbooks.
- Performance management.
- Absence management.
- Compliance support.
- Employment documentation.
- HR consultancy and retained services.

## Challenges

- Revenue tied to consulting hours.
- Clients increasingly expect software alongside advice.
- Difficulty differentiating from other consultancies.
- Limited recurring revenue streams.
- Risk of clients moving to larger providers with technology offerings.
- Growing pressure to embrace AI and automation.

## Why They Are Good Partners

- Already have trusted client relationships.
- Regularly advise clients on HR processes and systems.
- Clients frequently ask for software recommendations.
- Can introduce Vesra as part of an existing service offering.
- Strong opportunity for recurring revenue and client retention.

## Ideal Size

- 50-500 client employees managed across the consultancy's client base.
- 20-200 active clients.
- 2-20 consultancy employees.
- Retained HR support model.

## Exclusions

Exclude from the active HR consultancy partner campaign:

- Solo consultants with only a handful of clients.
- Recruitment agencies.
- General business consultants.
- Firms with no ongoing client relationships.
- Consultancies that already operate their own HR software platform.
- Accountancies.
- Employment law firms.
- Generic professional services firms without a retained HR support model.

## Mission

Help HR consultancies thrive in the AI era.

## Core Value Proposition

Turn HR expertise into recurring software revenue.

## Outcomes

- More revenue.
- Better client retention.
- More value for clients.
- Stronger differentiation.
- Reduced reliance on billable hours.
- A more modern service offering.

## Belief

Clients increasingly expect software, self-service, and AI-powered experiences alongside professional advice.

## Required Fields

At minimum, each qualified row should have:

- Company name.
- Segment.
- City or region.
- Website URL.
- Decision maker full name.
- Decision maker role.
- Decision maker LinkedIn URL where publicly verifiable.
- Email address.
- Email source URL.
- LinkedIn source URL or search evidence.
- Fit score.
- Status.

## Email Standards

Prefer public, source-backed business emails:

- Email published on the company website.
- Email published on a team profile page.
- Email published in a public directory or professional profile.
- Generic inbox, such as hello@ or info@, only if no named decision-maker email is available.

Avoid:

- Guessing personal email addresses without validation.
- Scraping private logged-in pages.
- Using non-business personal addresses.

If a likely email pattern is inferred, mark `email_confidence` as `low` unless validated by a source.

## LinkedIn Standards

Use public LinkedIn profile URLs where available. Do not automate logged-in LinkedIn actions. If the exact person profile cannot be verified, leave the field blank and record the search query or evidence in notes.

## Suggested Scoring

Score each company from 0 to 100:

- HR consultancy segment fit: 0-25.
- Retained/outsourced HR evidence: 0-20.
- SME/client fit: 0-15.
- Company size fit: 0-10.
- Decision-maker clarity: 0-10.
- Contactability: 0-10.
- Partnership relevance: 0-10.

Priority bands:

- 80-100: high priority.
- 60-79: medium priority.
- 40-59: low priority or research further.
- Below 40: exclude or park.
